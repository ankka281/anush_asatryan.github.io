A Letter from the Year 2095

Dear Grandma Anna,

I found your old letters and photographs today, tucked away in what's left of the coastal archives. The paper is brittle, the colors faded, but I can still see the ocean in your words‚Äîthe ocean as it was before The Collapse.

You wrote about diving with hammerhead sharks in 2032, their silhouettes dancing through schools of silver fish. Now they only exist in DNA banks. The coral reefs you studied? Gone by 2067, bleached then crumbled to dust. The Arctic tern migration you loved? The last nest was recorded in 2081.

I try to imagine the smell of salt without the undertone of petroleum from the '46 spill, the sound of waves without the constant hum of desalination plants. Your photos show beaches with actual sand, not the plastic granules that cover ours now.

But here's what I need you to know: we're trying. The Ocean Reclamation Projects are slow, but the artificial kelp forests are showing promise. Maybe by 2105, the water will be clear enough for someone to see what's left of the starfish.

I wish you could have saved it. We'll try to salvage what remains.

With love,
Your granddaughter
November 2095
